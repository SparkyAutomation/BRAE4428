Why HSV for tracking?
Hue stays constant even in shadow. A red ball looks different in RGB when shaded, but keeps hue ≈ 0° in HSV.
Why L*a*b* for skin?
Perceptually uniform — skin tones cluster tightly in a*b* space regardless of illumination.

Morphology Result

Erosion: A pixel is foreground only if ALL neighbors within SE are foreground. Removes isolated white noise.
Dilation: A pixel is foreground if ANY neighbor within SE is foreground. Fills small holes and gaps.
Opening: Erode then dilate. Removes thin protrusions and small objects without major shape change.
Closing: Dilate then erode. Fills small internal holes and closes narrow gaps.
False color maps intensity to a color spectrum, making subtle differences visible.
